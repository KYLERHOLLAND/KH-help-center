How to use

This pack is built for adjustment layers placed over your edit points.

Set up one transition

  1. In the Project panel, click New Item → Adjustment Layer and add it to a track above your clips.
  2. Move the playhead to the center of the cut you want to transition (use the Up and Down arrow keys to jump between edit points).
  3. Extend the adjustment layer on both sides of the cut. A common length is 80 frames total (40 frames each side): hold Shift and press Left Arrow eight times, trim the layer start to that point, jump to the other side of the cut, then Shift + Right Arrow eight times and trim the end.
  4. Drag any exposure preset from the Exposure bin onto the adjustment layer.
  5. Play back. Open Effect Controls to fine-tune keyframes, speed ramps, or channel sliders.

Shorter or longer layers change how fast the transition reads — the presets scale with the layer duration.

Reuse the same timing on other cuts

  1. Select your adjustment layer and press M to add a marker at the center.
  2. Hold Alt (Windows) or Option (Mac) and drag the layer to the next cut. The marker helps it snap to the new edit point.
  3. To swap presets, right-click the layer → Remove Attributes → OK, then drag on a different exposure preset.

Stack multiple presets

Drag more than one preset onto the same adjustment layer (or stack layers) to combine effects — for example, a Light preset plus a Unique preset for a custom composite look.

Choosing a preset

Naming Meaning
Quick Fast, punchy exposure change
Smooth Gradual fade in and out
Low / Medium / High Intensity of the exposure swing
Invert … Which color channel gets inverted (experiment in the Invert folder)
Unique … Specialty looks (Add RGB, Subtract, XOR, etc.)

Start with Dark or Light at medium intensity, then explore Invert and Unique once you're comfortable with timing.

Tips and workflow

  • Mark your layers. Press M on the adjustment layer so copied layers snap cleanly to other cuts.
  • Remove before swapping. Use Remove Attributes instead of deleting effects one by one when trying alternatives.
  • Match layer length to pacing. Fast cuts often work with shorter layers; cinematic sections can use longer ramps.
  • Customize in Effect Controls. All presets are modular — drag keyframes and sliders until the transition fits your footage.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need adjustment layers?

Yes. The tutorial workflow places presets on an adjustment layer above your clips so the effect spans the cut without altering source footage directly.

Can I apply the same look to multiple cuts quickly?

Yes. Add a center marker (M), Alt/Option-drag the layer to each cut, Remove Attributes, then apply a new preset from the bin.

The preset bin didn't appear after import

Import KH Exposure Transitions.prfpset (not the .zip). Right-click Presets → Import Presets → select the .prfpset → Open, then expand Kyler Holland presets → Transitions → Exposure.
