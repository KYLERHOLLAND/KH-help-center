## Installation

1. Extract the `.zip` to a dedicated folder (keep clips organized by name).
2. In Premiere, **Import** the `.mov`/`.mp4` files into a **Light Leaks** bin.

---

## How to use

1. Place a leak clip on a track **above** your footage.
2. Trim to the frames you need (often 6–15 frames for a transition punch).
3. Change **blend mode** (commonly **Screen** or **Add**) so blacks drop out and light blooms over the image.
4. Adjust **opacity** and optionally **Tint** in Effect Controls.
5. For custom leaks, film against **black** with a **zoom lens**, rack zoom during record (tutorial demo on a black couch/background).

---

## Tips and workflow

- **4K on 1080 timelines** — scale to frame; you get extra sharpness for cropping.
- **Don't cover the whole edit** — leaks work best as accents, not constant overlays.
- **Stack with grades** — apply [LUTs](10-cinematic-luts/index.md) on the base clip, leak above.
