## Installation

1. Download and **extract** the `.zip` to a folder you can keep (the tutorial stresses a location you'll remember).
2. Open Premiere Pro → **Effects** panel → right-click **Presets** → **Import Presets**.
3. Select the Fake Camera Movements `.prfpset` → **Open**.

---

## How to use

1. Select a **clip** you want to feel more dynamic (static tripod shots, locked-off drone frames, etc.).
2. Drag a **Fake Camera Movement** preset from the bin onto the clip.
3. Play back and open **Effect Controls** to tweak position, scale, or rotation keyframes the preset added.
4. Try different presets on adjacent clips for variety (subtle on wide shots, stronger on close-ups).

Unlike cut-based transition packs, these are meant to sell **camera motion within a single clip**.

---

## Tips and workflow

- **Layer subtle moves** on b-roll while keeping talking-head clips steadier.
- **Adjust end keyframes** if a move overshoots — all presets remain editable in Effect Controls.
- **Works at any resolution** — scale keyframes are relative to the frame size.
