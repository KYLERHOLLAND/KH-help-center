### Step 2 — Import into Premiere Pro

1. Open **Adobe Premiere Pro** and your project.
2. Go to the **Effects** panel.
3. Right-click **Presets** and select **Import Presets**.
4. Select the `.prfpset` file from the extracted folder.
5. Click **Open**.

A new preset bin appears in your Presets folder.

## How to use

### Recommended workflow

1. Create an adjustment layer the size of your sequence (for example, 1920×1080).
2. Place it over your cut point.
3. Move **10 frames** before and **10 frames** after the cut (Shift + Left/Right Arrow twice each way).
4. Add a marker at the center for easy snapping.
5. Drag a shake preset from **Shake Random**, **Shake Horizontal**, or **Shake Vertical** onto the adjustment layer.
6. Copy the adjustment layer to reuse the timing with different shake presets.
