## How to use

This pack works on **adjustment layers** centered on your cuts.

### Build one reusable shake layer

1. Create an **adjustment layer** matching your sequence size (for example **1920×1080**).
2. Place it on a track above your clips.
3. Jump to the **middle of a transition** with **Up/Down** arrow keys (moves between cuts).
4. From the center, hold **Shift** and press **Left Arrow** twice (**10 frames** back). Trim the layer start to that point.
5. Jump to the center again, then **Shift + Right Arrow** twice (**10 frames** forward) and trim the end.
6. Press **M** at the middle of the layer to add a **marker** — when you copy the layer, it snaps to other cuts.
7. Drag a preset from **Shake Random**, **Shake Horizontal**, or **Shake Vertical** onto the layer.

**10–20 frames** total length is the sweet spot; the tutorial uses 10 frames each side.

### Copy to other cuts and swap presets

1. Hold **Alt** (Windows) or **Option** (Mac) and drag the adjustment layer to the next cut (marker helps alignment).
2. To change the shake, right-click the layer → **Remove Attributes** → **OK**, then drag a different preset from the bin.
3. Select multiple layers, **Remove Attributes** once, then apply new presets in batch if you're retiming a section.

### Fine-tune

Select the adjustment layer and open **Effect Controls** — adjust shake parameters or keyframes if you want more or less intensity without changing presets.

## Tips and workflow

- **Color-label layers** (for example green) so shake layers stand out in a busy timeline.
- **Stack adjustment layers** when clips overlap on multiple tracks.
- **Use Remove Attributes** instead of deleting effects manually when iterating.
- **Preview with loop** on the Program Monitor when comparing Random vs. Horizontal vs. Vertical on the same cut.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why isn't the shake centered on my cut?

Re-center with **Up/Down** arrows, rebuild the 10-frame handles with **Shift + Left/Right**, and keep the **M** marker at the midpoint before copying.

### Can I use this on clips directly?

The pack is designed for adjustment layers so one layer can sit over any cut without nesting clips.
