The presets are organized into three animation styles, each with **Zoom In** and **Zoom Out** options at different intensity levels.

### Zoom styles

| Style | Motion |
|-------|--------|
| **Linear** | Constant zoom speed from start to finish |
| **Ease Out** | Starts slow, ends fast |
| **Ease In** | Starts fast, ends slow |

### Zoom intensity

Each preset name includes a percentage (for example, `130%`, `135%`, `140%`, `150%`). That number controls how far the zoom travels:

- **Zoom In 150%** — scales from 100% to 150%
- **Zoom Out 150%** — scales from 150% back down to 100%

Higher percentages produce a more dramatic zoom. Lower percentages are more subtle.

## How to use

### Apply a preset to a single clip

1. In the **KH Speed Zoom Transitions** bin, open the style folder you want (**Linear**, **Ease Out**, or **Ease In**).
2. Choose **Zoom In** or **Zoom Out**, then pick an intensity (for example, `Zoom In Linear 130%`).
3. Drag the preset onto a clip in your timeline.

Premiere adds keyframes to the clip's **Transform** effect automatically. You can open the Effect Controls panel to inspect the keyframes if you want to see exactly what's happening.

### Apply a preset to multiple clips at once

1. Select all the clips you want to affect in the timeline (Shift-click or drag a selection box).
2. Drag a preset from the bin onto any one of the selected clips.

The effect is applied to every selected clip immediately.

### Apply to your entire timeline

Drag a preset onto each clip — or select all clips and apply in one go — to give your whole edit a consistent zoom rhythm. Play back the sequence to get a feel for how the motion reads across your project.

## Choosing the right preset

Not sure which style fits your edit? Test on a single clip first:

1. Place one clip on your timeline.
2. Set the Program Monitor to **Loop** (click the loop icon) so it replays continuously.
3. Drag different presets onto the clip and compare.

### Style comparison (at 150%)

| Preset | What you'll see |
|--------|-----------------|
| Zoom In Linear 150% | Steady, even zoom inward |
| Zoom Out Linear 150% | Steady, even zoom outward |
| Zoom In Ease Out 150% | Builds slowly, then punches in at the end |
| Zoom Out Ease Out 150% | Eases out slowly, then snaps away quickly |
| Zoom In Ease In 150% | Jumps in fast, then settles at the end |
| Zoom Out Ease In 150% | Zooms out quickly, then decelerates |

Use **Linear** for clean, mechanical motion. Use **Ease Out** for a cinematic build-up. Use **Ease In** for an energetic snap that lands softly.

## Removing presets

If you want to try a different effect or start over:

1. Select the clip(s) you want to reset.
2. Right-click and choose **Remove Attributes**.
3. Check **Transform** (this removes the zoom keyframes the preset added).
4. Click **OK**.

Your clips return to their original state so you can apply a different preset.

!!!tip Experiment freely
It's common to apply a preset, play it back, remove it, and try another style. The remove-attributes workflow makes it quick to iterate.
!!!

## Tips and workflow

- **Start subtle.** Try 130% or 135% before jumping to 150% — you can always go bigger.
- **Match style to pacing.** Fast cuts pair well with Ease In; slower, cinematic sections often suit Ease Out.
- **Mix directions.** Alternate Zoom In on one clip and Zoom Out on the next for variety.
- **Check keyframes.** Open Effect Controls → Transform to see the scale keyframes the preset created. You can fine-tune timing manually if needed.
- **Resolution independent.** These presets work at 1080p, 4K, and other resolutions.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need to keyframe anything manually?

No. Each preset applies pre-built Transform keyframes. Drag, drop, and you're done.

### Can I use these between two clips as a transition?

These presets are designed to be applied directly to individual clips, adding zoom motion within the clip itself. For transition-style effects between two shots, experiment with placing the preset on the outgoing or incoming clip depending on the look you want.

### Can I apply different presets to different clips?

Yes. Each clip is independent — mix Linear, Ease In, and Ease Out across your timeline however you like.

### The preset bin didn't appear after import

Make sure you imported `KH Speed Zoom Transitions.prfpset` (not the `.zip` file). Right-click **Presets** in the Effects panel → **Import Presets** → select the `.prfpset` file → **Open**. Expand the Presets folder to find the **KH Speed Zoom Transitions** bin.
