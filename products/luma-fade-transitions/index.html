How to use

Apply a continuous fade

  1. Place two clips next to each other on the timeline.
  2. Trim or overlap so the outgoing clip meets the incoming clip at the cut.
  3. Drag Lumafade Continuous Light to Dark (or Dark to Light) onto the first clip and extend it over the second clip.
  4. The length of the overlap is the transition duration — drag the preset edge farther for a longer fade.

Use pause-style fades

  1. Lengthen the clip overlap slightly so the hold is visible.
  2. Apply Lumafade Pause Dark to Light or Lumafade Pause Light to Dark.
  3. Open Effect Controls — you'll see keyframes that rise to a midpoint, hold, then finish. Drag those keyframes to customize timing.

Manual control

Apply Lumafade Manual Adjustment when you want to shape every part of the curve yourself in Effect Controls.

Tips and workflow

  • Clip overlap = duration. Unlike adjustment-layer packs, you control timing by how much the preset spans both clips.
  • Modular keyframes. Pause and manual presets are meant to be dragged and retimed in Effect Controls.
  • Footage matters. Some clips take fades more naturally than others — try continuous vs. pause variants on the same cut.
  • Manipulate blacks, whites, or both. The pack is built for classic luma-fade looks (fade to black, fade from white, etc.).

Frequently asked questions

Do I need an adjustment layer?

No. This pack is designed to work on clips at the cut point.

How do I make the transition longer?

Extend how far the preset overlaps the second clip, or lengthen the clip handles and retime keyframes in Effect Controls.
